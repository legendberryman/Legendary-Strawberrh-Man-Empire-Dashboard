📅 Sean's Daily Workflow
1
48–60 hours after upload: Click "+ 48h Data" in the top bar. Enter numbers from YouTube Studio. The app will automatically tell you what to do next.
2
Read the automatic decision: The app reads CTR vs AVD and gives you a specific action — not just a score.
3
If you changed the thumbnail: Wait 72 hours. Check CTR in Studio. Up = working. No change = change title too.
4
7 days after upload: Re-enter updated data. If score 60+ → mark Repost Candidate.
5
Before every Upload 3 / Upload 7 in the cycle: Check Repost Queue → pick #1 in the right category → post it.
🎯 Automatic Decision Logic — Why Each Action
CTR below avg AND AVD below avg→ Change Thumbnail first
CTR below avg AND AVD above avg→ Thumbnail problem only — change it
CTR above avg AND AVD below avg→ Hook/content problem — check first 5 seconds
The app compares each video against the average of its category (High-Energy, Healing, or Hour-Long). These are the only two numbers that matter in the first 48 hours:
✅ CTR above avg AND AVD above avg→ Repost Candidate — it hooks AND holds. Add to repost queue.
Why CTR + AVD together? CTR tells you if the thumbnail/title makes people click. AVD tells you if the content keeps them watching. You need both to diagnose the problem correctly.
🖼 Three Thumbnail Styles to Test
Style A — Clean (current)
Clear shot of performer, no heavy editing. Natural look. Good baseline.
Style B — Shadow Edit (current)
Background blurred, person has dark shadow outline. Carl's standard edit. Gets solid CTR.
Style C — Text Overlay ← TEST THIS MORE
Person slightly to one side, bold text on the left. Old videos using this got 8–10% CTR. High priority to test on new Healing videos.
For 1-Hour videos: Consider SEO-focused titles — "Study Music", "Sleep Aid", "Focus Deep Work" — these tap into search traffic, not just YouTube suggestions.
